About

I am a theoretical and computational physicist specialized in quantum computing, quantum many-body physics, and condensed matter systems. My research focuses on bridging fundamental quantum physics with practical implementations on near-term quantum devices. I use a combination of analytical methods, tensor-network techniques, and quantum circuit design to explore novel quantum phenomena and develop efficient quantum algorithms.

Currently, I am a Research Fellow at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore, where I work on digital quantum simulations and quantum circuit optimization for IBM superconducting hardware. My research interests span quantum transport, localization phenomena, topological properties, and the development of robust quantum algorithms for NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum) devices.

I believe in the power of rigorous theoretical understanding combined with pragmatic computational approaches to advance quantum science and technology.

Research Overview

"The behavior of things in the small scale is so fantastic!" – Richard Feynman

My research explores the collective quantum phenomena that emerge when many particles interact with one another. I investigate how to simulate and observe these phenomena on quantum computers, particularly focusing on near-term quantum devices with limited resources.

Key Research Areas:

Quantum Computing on NISQ Devices Designing and optimizing quantum circuits for current and near-future quantum computers. I focus on implementing quantum algorithms that can extract useful information from imperfect devices.
Digital Quantum Simulation Mapping condensed matter physics problems onto quantum hardware through careful circuit design. This bridges theoretical predictions and experimental implementations.
Quantum Many-Body Physics Studying quantum phase transitions, localization phenomena, and exotic quantum states in low-dimensional systems using both analytical and numerical approaches.
Flat-Band Systems & Topological Phenomena Investigating quantum transport in special lattice geometries where interactions dominate and topological properties become crucial.
Tensor-Network Methods Developing and applying efficient numerical techniques (DMRG, TEBD, TDVP) for classical simulation of quantum systems and ground state properties.

Research Highlights: Latest Work

Paper 1: Digital Quantum Simulation of Flat-Band Dynamics (2025)

M. K. Giri and P. Chen
In this work, we demonstrate how to digitally simulate flat-band systems—exotic quantum states where energy becomes independent of momentum—on near-term quantum computers. Flat bands are fascinating because interactions completely dominate their physics, making them ideal platforms for exploring novel quantum phenomena.

Key Contribution: We develop efficient quantum circuits that map the physics of tunable quantum transport in flat-band materials onto IBM superconducting qubits. The circuit designs minimize the number of two-qubit gates, critical for reducing noise on NISQ devices. Our simulations reveal transport suppression phenomena not easily accessible to classical computers.

Paper 2: Topological Quantum Walks on NISQ Devices (2025)

M. K. Giri and S. B. Mandal
Topological quantum walks represent a fascinating intersection of topology and quantum dynamics. We propose a protocol to realize these walks—where the walker's trajectory exhibits protection against disorder—on real quantum hardware.

Key Contribution: We design quantum circuits that implement topological quantum walks using a qubit-efficient encoding. The protocol is resilient to certain types of noise and demonstrates how topological protection can be harnessed in quantum algorithms. Experimental implementation on IBM Heron and other systems shows good agreement with theoretical predictions.

📊 Figure: Probability distribution of walker position showing topological protection—the walker avoids disorder-induced localization

Physica Scripta, vol. 100 (2025)
Paper 3: Flux-Enhanced Localization in Interacting Bosons (2024)

M. K. Giri, B. Paul, and T. Mishra
This study investigates a counterintuitive phenomenon: how artificial magnetic fields (gauge potentials) can actually enhance localization of quantum particles on a ladder geometry. Normally, we expect particles to spread out, but interactions and geometry conspire to trap them.

Key Contribution: We use DMRG and analytical methods to reveal a rich phase diagram exhibiting multiple localization transitions. The reentrant delocalization—where increasing the field strength first localizes, then delocalizes particles—opens new avenues for quantum control in ultracold atom experiments. Results have direct implications for quantum simulation platforms.

📊 Figure: Phase diagram showing localization transitions as a function of artificial gauge field strength and interaction strength

Code Repositories & Resources

Open-source code and resources developed during my research:

Quantum Physics Libraries

cDMRG: Continuous DMRG

Density-Matrix Renormalization Group implementation for continuous quantum systems. Extends DMRG beyond lattice systems to handle inhomogeneous potentials and time-dependent scenarios.

Language: Python · Dependencies: NumPy, scipy, TeNPy
→ GitHub Repository
QuBond: Quantum Many-Body Solver

Integrated toolkit for solving quantum many-body problems using MPS, TEBD, and TDVP. Includes support for bosons, fermions, and spins with arbitrary interactions.

Language: Python · Dependencies: TeNPy, QuTiP
→ GitHub Repository
LocalizationTools: Many-Body Localization

Comprehensive toolkit for studying localization phenomena in quantum systems. Includes routines for disorder averaging, level statistics, and entanglement analysis.

Language: Python · Dependencies: NumPy, Matplotlib
→ GitHub Repository

Quantum Circuit Libraries

QCircuit: Quantum Walk Implementation

Qiskit-based library for implementing quantum walks on IBM quantum processors. Includes circuit optimization and noise mitigation strategies for NISQ devices.

Framework: Qiskit, IBM Quantum · Python 3.8+
→ GitHub Repository
FlatBandSimulator: Digital Simulation Suite

Complete implementation of flat-band Hamiltonian digital simulation. Supports variational approaches (VQE) and Trotter-based evolution on superconducting qubits.

Framework: Qiskit, Qiskit-Aer · IBM Heron Compatible
→ GitHub Repository
TopologyCircuits: Topological Quantum States

Library for generating topological quantum states and anyonic excitations on quantum circuits. Includes braiding and measurement protocols.

Framework: Qiskit, Cirq · Supports Multiple Backends
→ GitHub Repository
VariationalQAlgorithms: VQE & QAOA

Optimized implementations of variational quantum algorithms tailored for condensed matter and many-body physics problems. Includes custom ansätze and hybrid classical-quantum solvers.

Framework: Qiskit Nature, PyQMR · Production Ready
→ GitHub Repository
