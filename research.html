Research

Quantum Dynamics, Simulation & Many-Body Physics

My research focuses on quantum many-body systems using theoretical modeling, numerical simulation, and quantum-circuit-based approaches. I study how interactions, topology, disorder, and external fields influence quantum transport, localization, and dynamical behavior in low-dimensional systems.

A major direction of my current work is digital quantum simulation on near-term quantum devices, where condensed matter models are mapped into quantum circuits and optimized for noisy superconducting hardware.

Key Research Areas

Digital Quantum Simulation

Quantum-circuit implementations of lattice and many-body models on NISQ quantum hardware.

Quantum Walks & Transport

Transport, localization, and pairing dynamics in interacting low-dimensional quantum systems.

Flat-Band & Topological Systems

Compact localization and tunable transport in flat-band and topological lattice geometries.

Localization Phenomena

Disorder and quasiperiodic driven localization transitions in quantum systems.

Scalable NISQ Algorithms

Noise-aware circuit optimization for larger spin and condensed matter models.

Many-Body Quantum Dynamics

Nonequilibrium dynamics, quantum correlations, and critical behavior beyond classical limits.
