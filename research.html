Research

Quantum Dynamics, Simulation & Many-Body Physics

My research focuses on quantum many-body systems using theoretical modeling, numerical simulation, and quantum-circuit-based approaches. I study how interactions, topology, disorder, and external fields influence quantum transport, localization, and dynamical behavior in low-dimensional systems.

A major direction of my current work is digital quantum simulation on near-term quantum devices, where condensed matter models are mapped into quantum circuits and optimized for noisy superconducting hardware.

01

Digital quantum simulation of flat-band dynamics

arXiv:2508.08734 (2025)

Here we present high-fidelity digital quantum simulations of flat-band and all-bands-flat lattices using tensor-network-based circuit compression. The compressed circuits reveal controllable quantum transport, where the all-bands-flat lattice acts as a quantum switch.

Flux-enhanced localization and reentrant delocalization

Phys. Rev. A 109, 043308 (2024)

We demonstrate how magnetic flux can enhance localization and induce reentrant delocalization in interacting bosonic quantum walks on ladder geometries.

03

Signatures of non-trivial pairing in two-component quantum walks

Phys. Rev. Lett. 129, 050601 (2022)

We obtain signatures of nontrivial on-site pairing in the quantum walk of strongly interacting two-component bosons in a one-dimensional lattice, showing how competing intra- and intercomponent interactions lead to bound-state formation.
