Research

Quantum many-body physics on classical and quantum computers.

My research explores collective quantum phenomena, quantum transport, topological behavior, localization, and practical implementations on near-term quantum devices.

Research overview

My research investigates how interacting quantum particles give rise to emergent collective behavior. I study how such physics can be simulated on quantum computers, especially near-term devices with limited resources and noise constraints.

Key research areas

  • Quantum Computing on NISQ Devices: Designing and optimizing circuits for current quantum computers.
  • Digital Quantum Simulation: Mapping condensed matter models onto quantum hardware.
  • Quantum Many-Body Physics: Phase transitions, localization, and exotic low-dimensional states.
  • Flat-Band & Topological Phenomena: Quantum transport in special lattice geometries.
  • Tensor-Network Methods: DMRG, TEBD, TDVP, and MPS approaches.
