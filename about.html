About Me

Quantum Simulation &
Many-Body Physics

I am a theoretical and computational physicist working at the intersection of quantum computing, condensed matter physics, and many-body quantum systems. My research focuses on digital quantum simulation, quantum transport, localization phenomena, and quantum circuit implementations on NISQ devices.

Currently, I am a Research Fellow at Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore, where I work on quantum simulations and circuit optimization for superconducting quantum hardware.

Signatures of non-trivial pairing in two-component quantum walks

Phys. Rev. Lett. 129, 050601 (2022)

This work identifies pairing signatures in interacting two-component bosonic quantum walks, showing how correlations and mass imbalance reshape transport in low-dimensional systems.

Flux-enhanced localization and reentrant delocalization

Phys. Rev. A 109, 043308 (2024)

We demonstrate how magnetic flux can enhance localization and induce reentrant delocalization in interacting bosonic quantum walks on ladder geometries.

Digital quantum simulation of flat-band dynamics
