About Me

I am a theoretical and computational physicist specialized in quantum computing, quantum many-body physics, and condensed matter systems. My research focuses on bridging fundamental quantum physics with practical implementations on near-term quantum devices. I use a combination of analytical methods, tensor-network techniques, and quantum circuit design to explore novel quantum phenomena and develop efficient quantum algorithms.

Currently, I am a Research Fellow at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore, where I work on digital quantum simulations and quantum circuit optimization for IBM superconducting hardware. My research interests span quantum transport, localization phenomena, topological properties, and the development of robust quantum algorithms for NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum) devices.

I believe in the power of rigorous theoretical understanding combined with pragmatic computational approaches to advance quantum science and technology.
